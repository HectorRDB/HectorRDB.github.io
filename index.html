Biography

I am a PhD student working on statistical transcriptomics methods in Sandrine Dudoit’s lab. I am collaborating with the He Lab, and with a Berkeley team lead by John Ngai as part of the NIH Brain Inititative, to apply those statistical and computing methods to current biological questions. I am also working on a joint project between Pendulum and Laurent Jacob, developing metagenomics-related methodologies.

May 2019

Trajectory-based differential expression analysis for single-cell sequencing data

Trajectory inference has radically enhanced single-cell RNA-seq research by enabling the study of dynamic changes in gene expression levels during biological processes such as the cell cycle, cell type differentiation, and cellular activation. Downstream of trajectory inference, it is vital to discover genes that are associated with the lineages in the trajectory to illuminate the underlying biological processes. Furthermore, genes that are differentially expressed between developmental/activational lineages might be highly relevant to further unravel the system under study.
