Ressources for 'omics analysis

Apr 18, 2018

[THIS IS A DRAFT]

Overview

Recently, a new member joined the Dudoit lab and I had to think on which ressources to point him to so that he could start getting up to speed with on the field. I think that the ressources I recommand fall into 4 broad (and largely overlapping) categories:

  • Biology and techniques
  • How to find, acess and pre-process data (including how to use bio-informatics tools)
  • Statistics
  • How to use all of the above to conduct an analysis (with - of course - a strong emphasis on R tools)

I’ll therefore try to list ressources to help in all subjects. This will be a mix of (scientific) papers, books, package vignette, personal tutorials, video…

If you find any other ressources, feel free to contact me.

Before we begin

Those ressources assume that you have at least a basic knowledge of R, statistics and biology. For R otherwise, I recommand (and I am far from the only one) Garrett Grolemund & Hadley Wickham’s R For Data Science. Any suggestions for biology and statistics introductory guides are weclome.
